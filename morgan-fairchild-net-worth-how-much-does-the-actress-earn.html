How Did Morgan Fairchild Became Famous?

Morgan Fairchild began her acting career in 1967 as a double for Faye Dunaway in “Bonnie and Clyde.” She adopted the name “Morgan” after being inspired by the film “Morgan.” Moving to New York, she gained early success playing Jennifer Pace on the soap opera “Search for Tomorrow” from 1973 to 1977.

Fairchild appeared in various TV shows like “Kojak,” “Happy Days,” “Police Woman,” and “The Bob Newhart Show.” In 1978, she briefly joined “Dallas” and had a recurring role on “Mork & Mindy.” She later starred in “Flamingo Road,” earning a Golden Globe nomination.

After “Flamingo Road,” Fairchild had guest roles in shows like “Hotel,” “Simon & Simon,” “Magnum, P.I.,” and “The Love Boat.” She entered the big screen with “The Seduction” and gained fame hosting “Blondes vs. Brunettes.”

In the 80s, Fairchild appeared in soap operas like “Paper Dolls” and “Falcon Crest” and the miniseries “North and South.” She earned an Emmy nomination for “Murphy Brown” and had recurring roles in “Roseanne,” “The City,” and “General Hospital.” During the 90s, she made guest appearances on “Friends” and “Cybill.”

In the 2000s, Fairchild was featured in series like “Fashion House” and “Chuck,” and in 2009, she joined “The Bold and the Beautiful.” Throughout the 2010s, she continued with roles in “Bones,” “Revenge,” and “Hot in Cleveland.”
